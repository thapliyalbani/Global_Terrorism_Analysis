# Global_Terrorism_Analysis(1970-2017)
# Project Summary
The Global Terrorism Data Analysis project focuses on studying terrorist incidents recorded worldwide between 1970 and 2017 using the Global Terrorism Database (GTD). This dataset contains more than 180,000 terrorist incidents and provides detailed information about attack dates, locations, methods used, weapons involved, casualties, and the overall impact of each incident. The purpose of this project is to analyze terrorism trends, identify patterns, and understand the factors that contribute to the frequency and severity of attacks across different regions of the world.

The project begins with data preprocessing and cleaning, which is one of the most important steps in data analysis. Missing values in important columns such as the number of people killed (nkill) and wounded (nwound) were replaced with zero to ensure accurate numerical analysis and visualization. Special values such as -9 in the property damage column were treated as unknown values and removed to avoid misleading results. Ransom-related fields were also handled carefully whenever available in the dataset.

The analysis was divided into four major sections: temporal analysis, geographic analysis, attack and weapon analysis, and casualties with impact analysis. In temporal analysis, the number of terrorist attacks was studied year by year to understand how terrorism has evolved over time. The results showed a significant rise in attacks after the year 2000, with peak activity observed around 2014–2015. This indicates that terrorism became more intense and widespread in the 21st century.

Geographic analysis focused on identifying the most affected countries, regions, and cities. Countries such as Iraq, Afghanistan, and Pakistan were found to have the highest number of attacks, while regions like the Middle East, North Africa, and South Asia showed the greatest concentration of terrorism. City-level analysis also revealed repeated hotspots where attacks occurred frequently, proving that terrorism is often concentrated in specific conflict-prone areas rather than being evenly distributed worldwide.

The project also examined attack methods and weapon usage. Bombings and explosions were identified as the most common forms of terrorist attacks, followed by armed assaults and assassinations. Similarly, explosives and firearms were the most frequently used weapons. This shows that terrorist groups often prefer high-impact methods that can cause large-scale fear, destruction, and casualties.

Casualties and impact analysis included the study of deaths, injuries, property damage, and ransom demands. It was observed that most attacks resulted in a relatively small number of casualties, but a few major incidents caused extremely high fatalities and injuries. Property damage was found to be a frequent outcome of attacks, showing that terrorism affects not only human lives but also infrastructure and economic stability. Ransom-related incidents highlighted the financial motives behind certain attacks, especially in cases involving kidnappings and hostage situations.

Various visualization techniques were used to present the findings clearly, including line graphs, bar charts, pie charts, heatmaps, scatter plots, box plots, and histograms. These graphs helped in identifying trends, comparisons, relationships, and outliers in the dataset.

In conclusion, this project provides a comprehensive understanding of global terrorism patterns over several decades. It highlights the growing intensity of terrorism, the concentration of attacks in specific regions, the dominance of explosive-based attacks, and the severe human and economic consequences of such incidents. This analysis can be useful for researchers, policymakers, and security agencies in understanding terrorism and improving preventive strategies.


# Problem Statement
Terrorism remains one of the most serious global challenges, affecting human lives, national security, economic stability, and social peace across the world. Understanding the patterns, causes, and impact of terrorist incidents is essential for governments, researchers, and security agencies to develop effective prevention and response strategies. However, due to the large volume of incidents occurring across different countries and years, it becomes difficult to manually identify meaningful trends and hidden patterns.

The Global Terrorism Database (GTD) contains detailed records of more than 180,000 terrorist incidents from 1970 to 2017, including information about attack dates, locations, attack types, weapons used, casualties, property damage, and ransom demands. Despite the availability of such rich data, extracting useful insights requires systematic analysis and visualization.

The problem addressed in this project is to analyze the global terrorism dataset to identify major trends in terrorist activities over time, determine the most affected countries and regions, study the most common attack methods and weapons, and evaluate the human and economic impact of these incidents. The project aims to transform raw terrorism data into meaningful insights through data preprocessing, exploratory data analysis, tabular summaries, and multiple visualizations.

By understanding where attacks are concentrated, which methods are most frequently used, and how severe their consequences are, this project helps in building a clearer picture of global terrorism patterns. These insights can support better decision-making in security planning, policy formulation, and risk assessment.

# Business Objective
The primary business objective of this project is to analyze global terrorism data to identify patterns, trends, and high-risk areas that can support better decision-making for governments, security agencies, policymakers, and international organizations. Terrorism causes significant human loss, property destruction, economic instability, and social disruption, making it essential to study these incidents systematically.

By using the Global Terrorism Database (1970–2017), this project aims to understand how terrorist activities have changed over time, which countries and regions are most affected, what attack methods and weapons are most commonly used, and how severe the impact is in terms of casualties, property damage, and ransom demands.

The analysis helps in identifying terrorism hotspots, understanding common attack strategies, and evaluating the overall impact of attacks. These insights can assist organizations in improving resource allocation, strengthening security measures, developing preventive strategies, and planning emergency response systems more effectively.

Additionally, the project supports long-term strategic planning by helping decision-makers recognize recurring patterns and emerging threats. It also provides a data-driven foundation for risk assessment and policy formulation, enabling institutions to reduce vulnerabilities and improve public safety.

In summary, the business objective is to transform raw terrorism data into meaningful insights that can contribute to better security management, informed policy decisions, and more effective counter-terrorism strategies.
